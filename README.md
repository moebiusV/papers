## [12 Hz AC Power Grid for national sovereignty](papers/12hz-power-grid/)

**Commodity-Steel Transformers at 12 Hz: Breaking the GOES Bottleneck**
*Using Proven Bahnstrom Principles — March 2026*

The global electrical grid faces a binding supply-chain constraint: the shortage caused by
limited supply of GOES (grain-oriented electrical steel), required for transformer cores
operating at 50/60 Hz, produced by fewer than a dozen mills worldwide. The resulting
transformer backlog is throttling grid expansion in every industrialized and developing nation
simultaneously. Modern large transformers compound the problem: physically enormous, requiring
specialized transport, installation, and maintenance, they represent fragile single points of
failure across entire regions. This paper proposes a solution grounded in 113 years of
operational precedent: low-frequency AC grid architecture at 12 Hz, using commodity mild steel.
At 12 Hz, transformer cores can be wound from ordinary steel produced by hundreds of mills
worldwide. A 12 Hz transformer factory can be commissioned in 6–9 months. The result is
modular, locally manufacturable, and easier to install and maintain in every dimension.

## [Entfesselt](papers/entfesselt/)

**Vom Wagner zum Walther: Siegfried Entfesselt**
*Liberating Old Norse from Italian Grammar — 2026*

The Germanic languages are structurally closer to each other than their standard written forms
suggest. The distance between them is largely an artifact of 18th- and 19th-century prescriptive
standardization campaigns, each modeled on Latin or French, that suppressed shared features and
strengthened divergent ones. This paper identifies those suppressions systematically across the
family, proposes a rule-based methodology (Entfesselt, "unfettered") for reversing them, and
demonstrates the result: when the prescriptive impositions are removed, the family resemblance
becomes visible in every sentence. The methodology is applied to German in detail, with
extensions to Dutch, Danish, Swedish, Norwegian, Icelandic, Faroese, Frisian, Afrikaans,
Yiddish, Plautdietsch, and Scots. None of the languages have diverged as far from their
Old Norse, Old English, and Gothic origins as we have been led to believe.
