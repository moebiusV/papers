# 12 Hz AC Power Grid

Low-frequency AC grid architecture using commodity steel for large power transformers.
Modern high-voltage transformers are problematic in every dimension: the shortage caused
by limited supply of GOES (grain-oriented electrical steel) imposes lead times of 2–3 years,
but the problems go deeper. These machines are physically enormous, require specialized
transport and installation, depend on a thin global supply chain of expert manufacturers,
and represent single points of failure for entire regions. 12 Hz operation eliminates the
dependency on specialty steel, long supply chains, and expensive engineering talent — an
approach with 113 years of operational precedent in European railway electrification.
The result is modular, locally manufacturable, easier to install, and easier to maintain
in every dimension.
